<h3 align="center">Profile</h3>

      <td>
       I am Pinkrah Nana Ofosuhene, a Teaching and Research Assistant in the Department of Meteorology and Climate Science at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), Ghana. My passion lies in understanding and addressing the impacts of climate change, with a particular focus on climate dynamics in tropical regions, boundary layer meteorology, and weather forecasting.

I am currently researching heatwave trends in Ghana, examining their intensity under different climate scenarios such as RCP 8.5 and RCP 2.6. My analysis spans multiple regions, including Bole, Navrongo, Wa, Walewale, and Yendi, with the goal of understanding how future climate conditions will affect the frequency and severity of heatwaves.

Additionally, I have developed an SEIR model for HIV/AIDS dynamics in Ghana, ensuring the model accurately reflects real-world trends and constraints. This project demonstrates my commitment to applying data-driven methods to tackle real-world challenges. My repositories contain further details on my research and modeling work.

Beyond academia, I serve as a facilitator at Gumption Ex, where I teach programming languages like Python and C++. I use these skills in data analysis, visualization, climate simulations, and robotics projects, such as programming traffic lights and simulating vehicle movements with Arduino boards. Mentoring others in these areas is a passion, as I enjoy fostering a community of learning and innovation.
